##Interpretation of the coefficients For example, look at the coefficient for Agriculture. It is -0.17. We can interpret it as: our model estimates an expected 0.17 decrease in standarized fertility for every 1% increase in percentage of males involved in agriculture in holding the remaining variables constant.*

##What happen if we take only one of the variables as a predictor of the Ferlity For example, let's fit the model taking only the variable Agriculture as the regressor:

summary(lm(Fertility ~ Agriculture, data = swiss))$coefficients
